# Sourcing

## Source Strategy

Use the source strategy that fits the job:

- User assets: prioritize clips, images, logos, scripts, and voice files the user provides.
- Fresh web footage: search dynamically for the current topic. Good sources include stock sites, official websites, press rooms, YouTube channels, X/Twitter posts, Douyin/TikTok, Bilibili, Vimeo, event pages, and public media kits.
- Generated visuals: use when real footage is unavailable, unsafe to reuse, or the desired scene is conceptual.
- Screen capture: use for product demos, websites, apps, dashboards, walkthroughs, and tutorials.
- Existing video edit: if the user provides a video, preserve useful parts and only source extra media when it improves the result.

Respect platform terms, robots restrictions, login walls, and copyright. Record source URLs, uploader/account, publish date when visible, license or permission notes, and any reuse caveat in `footage_manifest.md` or `sources.md`. If direct download is blocked, record the source and use a legal alternate.

For web-sourced videos, make a fresh `shot_plan.md` before downloading:

- segment or timestamp
- desired visual evidence, action, or metaphor
- search queries for this specific job
- candidate platforms
- selected clip URL and reason
- fallback source

## Free Footage Source Index

Treat these as candidate sources, not blanket permission. Verify license and reuse terms for each selected clip, record attribution requirements, and avoid unclear provenance, watermarks, login-only downloads, or commercial-use restrictions that conflict with the brief.

Best default sources:

- Pexels Videos: `https://www.pexels.com/videos/` - broad free stock footage for people, lifestyle, business, vertical clips, and quick social b-roll.
- Pixabay Videos: `https://pixabay.com/videos/` - broad stock footage for nature, city, abstract, tech metaphors, and large search coverage.
- Mixkit: `https://mixkit.co/free-stock-video/` - polished free clips for cinematic, creator, product, and mood-setting footage.
- Coverr: `https://coverr.co/` - polished clips for brand-style openers, website backgrounds, startup/product atmosphere, and clean establishing shots.
- Videvo: `https://www.videvo.net/` - free plus paid footage; check whether the selected free clip requires attribution.
- Videezy: `https://www.videezy.com/` - free plus paid footage; check attribution and commercial-use terms per clip.
- Mazwai: `https://mazwai.com/` - cinematic curated footage; many clips use CC-style licenses, so record attribution when required.
- Dareful: `https://www.dareful.com/` - 4K-focused free footage; may require registration or license checks.
- Life of Vids: `https://www.lifeofvids.com/` - smaller curated free stock footage library.
- Vidsplay: `https://www.vidsplay.com/` - free stock footage; check attribution requirements.
- Free Nature Stock: `https://freenaturestock.com/` - nature, landscape, sky, water, and outdoor atmosphere.
- Dissolve Free Footage: `https://dissolve.com/` - high-quality stock; only use explicitly free or properly licensed items.

Effects, alpha, and motion-background sources:

- FootageCrate: `https://footagecrate.com/` - VFX elements, overlays, keyed effects, action hits, and social-style effects; check free limits and license.
- Ignite Motion: `https://ignitemotion.com/` - looping motion backgrounds, especially tech, space, abstract, and presentation backgrounds.
- Movie Tools: `https://movietools.info/` - looping video backgrounds, 3D motion loops, and general motion graphics.
- CuteStockFootage: `https://cutestockfootage.com/` - stock footage, effects, textures, and motion elements; check CC/license notes.

Template and mixed-media sources:

- MotionElements: `https://www.motionelements.com/` - stock footage, templates, music, SFX, and free assets; check item-level license and account requirements.
- Magnific / Freepik Video: `https://www.magnific.com/cn/视频` - free plus paid video resources; free items may require attribution.
- Envato Elements: `https://elements.envato.com/` - subscription library; use only when the user has access or the asset is free.

Chinese-language and domestic sources:

- 爱给网: `https://www.aigei.com/video/` - video, SFX, AE/Premiere templates, and design materials; many items are partial-free or account-gated.
- 新CG儿: `https://www.newcger.com/` - AE/Premiere templates and domestic editor-friendly resources.
- 觅元素: `https://www.51yuansu.com/` - cutout and transparent-channel design/video elements; verify license and watermark status.
- 摄图网视频: `https://www.699pic.com/video/` - large domestic stock library; check free quota, license, and commercial-use terms.
- 千图网: `https://www.58pic.com/` - images, video, and templates; check free quota, license, and commercial-use terms.
- Vamk: `https://www.vamk.cn/` - AE/Premiere templates and domestic video-template resources.
- VJ师网 / 光厂: `https://www.vjshi.com/` - domestic video, motion graphics, and template marketplace; use only properly licensed assets.
- 影视飓风素材库: `https://www.ysjf.com/material` - creator-oriented domestic footage/material library; check item-level license, free quota, and commercial-use terms.

## Fast Routing

- Daily social editing: Pexels + Pixabay first, then Mixkit for polished clips.
- Commercial or brand-feeling projects: Mixkit + Coverr first, then official press kits or product pages.
- Technology, AI, finance, or abstract explainers: Pixabay + Mixkit + Coverr, plus Ignite Motion or Movie Tools for subtle data/space/network backgrounds.
- Effects, compositing, overlays, and alpha assets: FootageCrate + CuteStockFootage first.
- AE/Premiere template needs: MotionElements + 爱给网 + 新CG儿 + Vamk.
- Chinese interface or domestic-topic videos: 爱给网 + 新CG儿 + 摄图网 + VJ师网 + 影视飓风素材库, with license checks per item.
- Large free-footage search pass: Pexels + Pixabay + Dareful, then broaden to Videvo/Videezy/Mazwai when attribution is acceptable.
